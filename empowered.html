EmpowerED: Opening Doors to Higher Education for Refugee Students

How one student's college journey inspired her to create pathways for Rohingya refugee students navigating the impossible transition to university

Bridging the gap between displacement and opportunity through education

üåç The Invisible Barrier

In Malaysia's southern region of Johor, thousands of refugee students face a paradox that defines modern displacement: they can access basic education, but the pathway to higher education remains almost impossibly distant. For many in the Rohingya community, there exists a rare school that offers hope-a bridge to tertiary education in the Philippines-but the resources to actually cross that bridge are virtually nonexistent.

Imagine preparing for the most important transition of your life with nothing but instinct to guide you. No college counselors. No alumni networks. No comprehensive guides about different fields of study. No interview preparation. Just hope, determination, and the overwhelming uncertainty that comes with navigating unfamiliar systems while carrying the weight of displacement.

This is the reality for refugee students in Johor who dream of higher education. And it's a reality that Saanvi Kedia, a student in Singapore, couldn't accept.

üéì When Your Own Privilege Becomes Your Call to Action

Saanvi's journey to founding EmpowerED began with a moment of realization that changed everything. As she navigated her own college application process-researching universities, exploring different academic paths, preparing for interviews-she was surrounded by resources. Shared experiences from older students. Guidance counselors who understood the system. Access to information about every possible pathway.

As a student who is also navigating her own college journey, I have relied so much on shared resources. I can't imagine going through this process without the same kind of access and freedom to make decisions.

That privilege became impossible to ignore. While she had the luxury of exploring options and making informed choices, students just across the border in Johor faced their futures with only instinct and limited information. The inequity wasn't just unfair-it was preventable.

üöß The Reality Refugee Students Face:

  • Limited pathway awareness: Many don't even know what fields of study exist
  • Zero soft skills training: No preparation for interviews, networking, or professional communication
  • Resource scarcity: Minimal access to college application guidance or comparative information
  • Isolation: No alumni network or mentors who understand their unique circumstances
  • Decision paralysis: Without information, life-changing choices become shots in the dark

üí° Building Bridges Through Information

EmpowerED's approach is as practical as it is transformative. Rather than trying to solve every challenge refugee students face, Saanvi focused on what she knew could create immediate impact: democratizing access to information and skills.

The platform centers on two interconnected pillars:

üìö Pathway Discovery & College Navigation

EmpowerED creates comprehensive resources about different fields of study, college programs, and career pathways. Students can explore options they never knew existed, compare different academic routes, and make informed decisions about their futures. The platform translates complex educational systems into accessible information that empowers rather than overwhelms.

üéØ Essential Soft Skills Development

Beyond academic information, EmpowerED addresses the hidden curriculum that privileged students absorb through osmosis but refugee students rarely encounter:

  • Interview preparation: From college admissions to eventual job searches
  • Professional communication: Email etiquette, networking basics, self-presentation
  • Application strategies: How to tell your story effectively and authentically
  • Confidence building: Overcoming imposter syndrome and advocating for yourself

üåü The Power of Peer-to-Peer Learning

What makes EmpowerED particularly effective is Saanvi's position as a fellow student. She's not speaking from the distant perspective of an adult who completed university decades ago-she's navigating the same challenges in real-time. This creates a unique credibility and relatability that traditional educational programs often lack.

Students aren't just receiving information; they're learning from someone who understands exactly what they're going through.

üöÄ From Idea to Impact: The Launch Journey

Founded in 2025 and based in Singapore, EmpowerED is preparing for its official launch with a clear mission: ensure that every refugee student, regardless of background, has access to the same information and opportunities that students in better-resourced communities take for granted.

The platform's initial focus is the Rohingya refugee community in Johor, Malaysia-students who have demonstrated incredible resilience in pursuing education despite immense obstacles. But the vision extends beyond geography. Saanvi is already considering expansion into India, recognizing that educational inequity affects displaced communities globally.

Measured Success Through Student Engagement

EmpowerED defines success not through abstract metrics but through tangible student outcomes:

  • Exploration and discovery: Students encountering pathways they wouldn't have otherwise known existed
  • Confident decision-making: Informed choices about academic directions and career paths
  • Skill acquisition: Practical abilities that ease the transition from school to university to eventual employment
  • Reduced anxiety: The confidence that comes from preparation and information

üå± The Philosophy of Starting Now

When asked what advice she'd give to others who want to create change, Saanvi's response reflects the urgency that drives EmpowerED:

If you have an idea, start working on it as soon as possible. Once the momentum starts, it's easy to keep going.

This philosophy of action over endless planning has shaped EmpowerED's development. Rather than waiting for perfect conditions or comprehensive funding, Saanvi began building immediately-creating resources, establishing connections, and preparing to serve students who couldn't afford to wait.

The approach acknowledges a crucial truth: for students navigating college applications right now, "someday" isn't good enough. Each year of delay means another cohort of students making life-defining decisions without adequate support.

üîÆ Growing Impact, Expanding Reach

As EmpowerED prepares to launch, Saanvi is already thinking about sustainable growth and continuous improvement. The roadmap reflects both ambition and pragmatism:

Near-Term Goals:

  • Launch comprehensive resource library covering major fields of study and career pathways
  • Develop soft skills curriculum with practical, actionable guidance
  • Build direct relationships with refugee students in Johor
  • Gather continuous feedback to refine and improve resources
  • Create mentorship connections between current students and those ahead in their journeys

Long-Term Vision:

  • Geographic expansion: Bringing similar resources to refugee communities in India and beyond
  • Partnership development: Collaborating with existing refugee support organizations
  • Alumni network creation: Connecting students who've successfully navigated the process
  • Resource localization: Adapting materials for different contexts and educational systems
  • Scalable platform: Building technology infrastructure that can serve growing numbers of students

üí™ The Multiplier Effect of Educational Access

EmpowerED's impact extends far beyond individual college applications. When refugee students gain access to higher education with proper preparation and information, the ripple effects transform entire communities:

  • Economic mobility: Education creates pathways out of poverty and displacement
  • Community leadership: Educated individuals return to serve their communities
  • Intergenerational change: Parents who accessed education prioritize it for their children
  • Breaking cycles: Education disrupts patterns of disadvantage and limited opportunity
  • Representation matters: Success stories inspire the next generation to believe they can also achieve their dreams

üåü A Call to Shared Humanity

EmpowerED represents more than a platform or an initiative-it's a declaration that educational opportunity shouldn't depend on the circumstances of your birth. Refugee students possess the same potential, dreams, and determination as students anywhere in the world. They face the same anxieties about choosing the right major, preparing for interviews, and making life-defining decisions.

The only difference is access to information and support. And that difference, as Saanvi demonstrates, is entirely within our power to address.

By building EmpowerED while still navigating her own college journey, Saanvi embodies a crucial truth about social change: you don't need to have all the answers or wait until you're "qualified enough." You just need to recognize an injustice, commit to addressing it, and start building solutions now.
