Open Door: When AI Meets Compassion for Migrant Workers

How a student's daily observations transformed into an AI chatbot helping migrant workers access affordable resources in Singapore

Using technology to bridge the gap between those who build our cities and the resources they need

üë∑ The People Who Build Our Cities

Every day, millions of people pass by construction sites, cleaning crews, and delivery workers without a second thought. These migrant workers are the invisible backbone of modern Singapore-constructing skyscrapers, maintaining infrastructure, keeping businesses running. Yet for all their essential contributions, they remain largely unseen by the very society they help build.

For Tvisha Jhamb, a student in Singapore, these workers were impossible to ignore. Walking past her school every day, she witnessed their reality firsthand: workers sweating under the harsh sun, carrying heavy loads, pushing through physically demanding days. While others saw just another part of the urban landscape, Tvisha saw human beings struggling with challenges that extended far beyond their workday.

What she discovered through research would change her perspective forever-and ultimately lead to the creation of Open Door, an AI-powered solution addressing one of the most overlooked obstacles facing migrant workers in Singapore.

üîç The Hidden Struggle: Access Over Affordability

When Tvisha began investigating the challenges migrant workers face, she expected to find issues related to wages, working conditions, or housing-the problems typically highlighted in public discussions. What emerged instead was something more insidious precisely because it was so ordinary: the difficulty of accessing affordable everyday resources.

üöß The Resource Access Crisis:

  • Information barriers: Workers often don't know where affordable resources exist
  • Language obstacles: Navigation and communication in unfamiliar languages
  • Geographic isolation: Workers often housed far from affordable options
  • Time constraints: Long work hours leave minimal time to search for resources
  • Network limitations: Lack of local connections who know the area
  • Exploitative pricing: Convenience stores near worksites charge premium prices

Imagine finishing an exhausting 12-hour shift and needing to find affordable groceries, medical care, or basic supplies. You don't know the area. You have limited language skills. You're exhausted. Every minute spent searching is a minute stolen from the few hours of rest you have before starting again tomorrow. This wasn't just an inconvenience-it was a daily crisis that compounded the already difficult reality of migrant work.

I think seeing their struggles and looking at so many migrant workers near my school sweating and doing so much work-I wanted to do something for them. After researching a lot, I found that one of the most significant concerns was finding accessible resources, and hence I tried solving for that problem.

ü§ñ Technology as Bridge, Not Barrier

Tvisha's solution emerged from a crucial insight: while migrant workers face numerous challenges, most carry smartphones-the one tool that could potentially bridge the information gap. What they needed wasn't another complex system to navigate, but a simple, accessible way to find affordable resources nearby.

Enter Open Door: an AI-powered chatbot designed specifically for migrant workers in Singapore. Rather than creating another app that requires downloading, registration, and learning a new interface, Tvisha built a conversational assistant that works through familiar messaging platforms.

üåü How Open Door Works:

The chatbot uses artificial intelligence to understand worker queries and provide personalized recommendations for affordable resources based on their location and needs. It's designed to be:

  • Conversational: Natural language interaction, no technical knowledge required
  • Multilingual: Support for languages commonly spoken by migrant workers
  • Location-aware: Provides nearby options based on user's current location
  • Comprehensive: Covers groceries, healthcare, remittance services, and more
  • Constantly updated: Database regularly refreshed with new affordable options

The Power of Simple Questions, Powerful Answers

A worker can simply ask: "Where can I find cheap groceries near Jurong?" and receive immediate, relevant recommendations. Or: "I need to send money home-what's the cheapest option?" The AI understands context, remembers previous conversations, and learns to provide increasingly relevant suggestions.

üí° Example Interaction:

Worker: "I'm near Kallang MRT. Where can I buy affordable vegetables?"

Open Door: "There are 3 affordable options within 10 minutes: 1) Tekka Market (7 min walk) - cheapest fresh vegetables, open till 9pm. 2) FairPrice Finest (5 min walk) - budget section available. 3) Mustafa Centre (12 min walk) - bulk buying options, open 24/7."

‚ù§Ô∏è Building with Empathy, Not Assumptions

What distinguishes Open Door from typical technology solutions is its foundation in genuine empathy rather than assumptions about what workers need. Tvisha didn't build what she thought would be useful-she researched, observed, and designed around actual challenges migrant workers expressed.

This empathy-driven approach manifests in subtle but crucial design choices:

  • Simplicity first: No unnecessary features that complicate the core mission
  • Respect for dignity: Language that treats workers as valued community members, not charity cases
  • Privacy-conscious: Minimal data collection, no intrusive tracking
  • Offline capability: Key information cached for areas with poor connectivity
  • Cultural sensitivity: Understanding of dietary restrictions, remittance patterns, community preferences
I feel that they already have so much to do and so much workload that making their life easier by providing them resources can ease off some of their stress and workload.

üå± The Ripple Effect of Accessible Resources

When Tvisha describes her impact goal-"each migrant worker's life is made accessible by providing resources nearby that are affordable"-she's articulating something profound about dignity and agency. Open Door isn't just about saving a few dollars on groceries. It's about:

Time Reclaimed

Every hour not spent wandering unfamiliar neighborhoods searching for affordable options is an hour that can be spent resting, connecting with family back home, or pursuing personal interests. For workers with grueling schedules, time is the most precious resource.

Stress Reduced

Financial stress compounds the physical exhaustion of migrant work. Knowing where to find affordable essentials removes one layer of daily anxiety, creating mental space for workers to focus on their wellbeing and goals.

Agency Restored

Perhaps most importantly, Open Door provides choice. Instead of being trapped by circumstance-forced to use the expensive convenience store near the worksite because they don't know alternatives exist-workers gain the power to make informed decisions about their own resources.

üí≠ The Philosophy of Small Ideas, Big Changes

When asked what advice she'd offer others wanting to create change, Tvisha's response reveals a maturity beyond her years:

I would advise people to do anything they can for the betterment of society, and changes don't always come from grand inventions but from small ideas. Even like this simple chatbot I have created can give the migrant workers a choice, a way forward, and an area of upbringing.

This philosophy-that meaningful change comes from simple ideas executed with genuine care-challenges the prevalent narrative that social impact requires massive resources or revolutionary innovations. Open Door demonstrates that technology's highest purpose isn't complexity or novelty, but serving fundamental human needs with elegance and empathy.

The Danger of Waiting for "Big Enough"

Too often, people see problems and think, "I can't solve the whole thing, so why bother?" Tvisha's work proves this mindset wrong. She didn't wait until she could transform the entire migrant worker experience. She identified one specific, solvable problem-access to affordable resources-and built a practical solution.

The result? A tool that makes immediate, tangible differences in daily lives while demonstrating what's possible when we stop waiting for permission to help.

üîÆ Growing Impact, Sustainable Vision

Open Door launched in 2025, and Tvisha is already thinking strategically about expanding impact while maintaining quality. The roadmap reflects both ambition and pragmatism:

Near-Term Development:

  • Community partnerships: Collaborating with organizations that work directly with migrant workers
  • Database expansion: Adding more categories (education, legal services, recreation)
  • User feedback integration: Continuously improving based on worker input
  • Language support expansion: Adding more languages spoken by migrant communities
  • Awareness campaigns: Ensuring workers know the tool exists

Long-Term Vision:

  • Community-driven updates: Workers contributing recommendations and reviews
  • Skills marketplace: Connecting workers with training opportunities and side income
  • Social connection features: Helping workers find community and support networks
  • Advocacy insights: Using aggregated data (privacy-protected) to inform policy improvements

üåç Redefining Who Belongs in Our Communities

Open Door challenges us to reconsider who we see as part of our communities. Migrant workers aren't temporary visitors or invisible labor-they're community members with the same fundamental needs and deserve the same access to resources that we take for granted.

By creating a tool that treats workers with dignity and serves their actual needs, Tvisha demonstrates what inclusive technology looks like. It's not about building platforms that extract data or monetize vulnerable populations. It's about using technical skills to expand access, restore agency, and acknowledge shared humanity.

The Test of Our Values

How we treat those who build our cities, clean our streets, and maintain our infrastructure reveals the truth about our community values. Do we see them? Do we care about their struggles? Are we willing to use our privileges-education, technology access, local knowledge-to help?

Open Door answers these questions with a resounding yes, proving that one student with empathy and technical skills can create meaningful change.

üéØ Try Open Door

Whether you're a migrant worker searching for affordable options, someone who wants to contribute resource recommendations, or an organization that serves these communities, Open Door offers a practical way to make Singapore more accessible and equitable for everyone who helps build it.
